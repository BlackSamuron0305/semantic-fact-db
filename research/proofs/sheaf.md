# Sheaf

## Definition

A **sheaf** on a topological space $(X, \mathcal{T})$ is a presheaf
$F: \mathcal{T}^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$ that satisfies two
additional axioms:

### Locality

For every open set $U \subseteq X$ and every open cover
$\{U_i\}_{i \in I}$ of $U$, if $s, t \in F(U)$ are sections such that

\[
\rho_{U, U_i}(s) = \rho_{U, U_i}(t) \quad \text{for all } i \in I,
\]

then $s = t$.

### Gluing

For every open set $U \subseteq X$ and every open cover
$\{U_i\}_{i \in I}$ of $U$, if we have sections $s_i \in F(U_i)$ that
are **compatible** on overlaps — i.e. for all $i, j \in I$,

\[
\rho_{U_i, U_i \cap U_j}(s_i) = \rho_{U_j, U_i \cap U_j}(s_j),
\]

then there exists a **unique** section $s \in F(U)$ such that

\[
\rho_{U, U_i}(s) = s_i \quad \text{for all } i \in I.
\]

## Sheaf on a Poset (Alexandrov Case)

When $X$ is a poset $(C, \leq)$ with its Alexandrov topology, the opens
are down-sets.  A presheaf $F: C^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is a sheaf
iff for every $c \in C$ and every cover $\{c_i\}$ of $c$ (a set of
elements with $c_i \leq c$ whose down-sets cover the down-set of $c$),
the locality and gluing conditions hold.

In practice for SFDB, the cover is often generated by the **immediate
sub-contexts** of $c$ — i.e. contexts $c_i$ such that $c_i < c$ and
there is no $d$ with $c_i < d < c$.

## Interpretation for SFDB

The sheaf condition provides the **consistency guarantee**:

- **Locality** ensures that two facts that agree on every sub-context
  are identical.  This prevents storing contradictory specialised facts.
- **Gluing** ensures that facts stated in different sub-contexts can be
  combined into a single coherent fact in a broader context, provided
  they agree on overlaps.
