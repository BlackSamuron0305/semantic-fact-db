\section{Ablation Studies}
\label{sec:ablation}

To identify which components of SheafDB contribute most to end-to-end performance, we conduct a scoped ablation comparing three configuration profiles that span the practical optimization space:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Minimal} --- core tuple storage only; no caching, no indexing beyond the primary open-set index.
  \item \textbf{Stalk-indexed} --- default SheafDB with stalk indexing, restriction-map caching, and temporal index enabled.
  \item \textbf{Full} --- all available optimisations including provenance morphisms, interval indexing, and global section caching.
\end{itemize}

Each profile is evaluated on the representative workloads from Sections~\ref{sec:event_reconstruction} through~\ref{sec:consistency_global} at 10K-fact scale. The per-profile latency breakdown mirrors the LOOKUP/GLOBAL decomposition reported in Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}, with stalk indexing providing the dominant contribution on entity-centric lookups and the full profile adding marginal benefit on mixed workloads.

We leave a comprehensive 33-dimensional leave-one-out analysis---including the pairwise interaction sweeps and sensitivity surfaces described in the companion optimisation framework---for future work. The modular architecture of the SheafDB engine (Section~\ref{sec:system_architecture}) is designed to support such fine-grained ablation when larger-scale ground-truth data become available.
