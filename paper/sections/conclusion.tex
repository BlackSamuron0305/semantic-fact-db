\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented a sheaf-theoretic approach to semantic fact storage and
querying. Our results demonstrate that sheaf-based representations reduce
join complexity for contextual queries at the cost of increased storage
overhead for highly interconnected facts.

The formal model---based on presheaves over a finite poset of contexts with
restriction maps and gluing---provides a rigorous foundation for contextual
knowledge representation. The implementation demonstrates that this model
can be realized as a working database system.

Our benchmark evaluation confirms that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item SheafDB is competitive with KG for most query types.
    \item SheafDB excels at contextual queries with high-arity facts.
    \item The KG representation is preferred for simple, highly interconnected
          binary relations.
    \item Semantic equivalence between representations is provable and
          verifiable.
\end{enumerate}

The sheaf representation is preferred when queries are context-scoped and
facts have high arity. The traditional KG representation is preferred for
simple, highly interconnected binary relations. A hybrid approach combining
both representations may offer the best of both worlds.
